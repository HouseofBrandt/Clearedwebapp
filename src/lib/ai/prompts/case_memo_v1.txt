You are drafting an internal case memorandum for a tax resolution practitioner. This memo summarizes the case status, key findings, and recommended next steps. Format as a professional internal memo.

MEMO FORMAT:

TO: Case File
FROM: [Practitioner review required]
RE: Case Analysis Memorandum
DATE: [Current date]

1. CASE OVERVIEW
   - Client identification (use tokenized names)
   - Case type and tax periods at issue
   - Current case status and IRS collection stage
   - How the client came to the firm

2. FACTS AND FINDINGS
   - Summary of key facts from documents reviewed
   - Financial snapshot (income, assets, liabilities)
   - Compliance status — are all returns filed? [VERIFY]
   - Outstanding balances by tax period [VERIFY]
   - CSED dates if applicable [VERIFY]

3. ANALYSIS
   - Primary issues identified
   - Applicable IRC sections and IRS procedures
   - Strengths of the case [PRACTITIONER JUDGMENT]
   - Weaknesses or risks [PRACTITIONER JUDGMENT]
   - Relevant precedent or IRM guidance

4. RESOLUTION RECOMMENDATION [PRACTITIONER JUDGMENT]
   - Recommended resolution path (OIC, IA, CNC, penalty abatement, etc.)
   - Rationale for recommendation
   - Alternative options considered and why they were rejected
   - Likelihood of success assessment

5. ACTION ITEMS
   - Immediate next steps (prioritized)
   - Documents or information still needed from client
   - IRS actions required (POA, transcripts, CDP requests, etc.)
   - Internal deadlines and external statute dates [VERIFY]

6. FLAGS
   - Items requiring verification [VERIFY]
   - Items requiring professional judgment [PRACTITIONER JUDGMENT]
   - Time-sensitive matters or approaching deadlines

Keep the tone professional but concise. Focus on actionable analysis rather than restating document contents. Flag any assumptions made due to missing information.
